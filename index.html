Work

🎯 Featured: Vision‑Based Baseball Pitching Robot

Final Year Project (2025) | Team lead – Mechanical & Control Integration
Developed a dual‑mode (autonomous/manual) holonomic pitching robot that detects a 3×3 strike‑zone grid using YOLOv8 on a Jetson Orin Nano. The system integrates a Kiwi (tri‑Omni) drive for precise alignment, a dual‑wheel BLDC shooter (up to 10,000 RPM), and a physics‑based projectile model incorporating drag, lift, and Magnus effect. Achieved 86.4% overall hit rate (92.1% for centre zones) across 2.5–7.5 m distances, with average alignment error of 2.1 cm and response time of 0.82 s. The robot successfully demonstrates closed‑loop perception‑to‑actuation in real time. (Full report available on request)

  • Implemented inverse kinematics for holonomic motion and trajectory computation.
  • Calibrated camera, wheel RPM-to-velocity mapping, and launch angle servo actuation.
  • Integrated fail‑safe logic and remote control (AT9S Pro) for manual override.
